Design notes

Geometry is centered at (150,150). The outer ring (#compass-ring) is r=130, stroked 1.2pt at 40% white. Minor ticks (#ticks-minor) span r=126→130 (4pt long, 0.7pt stroke, 40% white); major ticks (#ticks-major) span r=122→130 (8pt long, 1.1pt stroke, 55% white). All ticks are stroke-linecap="round" — the rounded end softens the perimeter without thickening it.

Cardinals (#cardinals, 13pt SF Pro semibold, pure white, centered) sit at r=115 — exactly 15pt inside the rim, with their bounding box clear of the major-tick interior end (r=122) by ~3pt of optical air. Numeric bearings (#bearings, 9pt SF Mono, white@60%) sit at r=113. Both groups use text-anchor="middle" + dominant-baseline="central" so labels are mathematically centered on the radial axis rather than baseline-tangent — this reads cleaner at small sizes than rim-tangent rotation.

Vectors share the same line weight (1.6pt) so color, not stroke, distinguishes them. Airspeed scale: 0.82 px/kt (TC reaches r=95; TH r=98.4). Wind uses a separate 2.0 px/kt scale (15kt → 30pt) so the arrow isn't swallowed by its own 11pt head — flag this in your Canvas constants. Arrowheads (#vector-* <polygon>) are isosceles, 11pt long × 6pt wide, fill-only — line ends at the tip; the polygon overlays. Center dot (#center-dot) is r=3, solid white. Legend (#legend) sits at x=14, baselines 266/278/290, 9pt SF Mono in the matching accent.

Variant B layers three additions onto identical geometry: #true-north-notch (a 6×6pt filled triangle at y=9–15, just outside the ring at 0°) so true-north is unambiguous even with the rose rotated to a course-up frame later; a feGaussianBlur stdDeviation=1.6 on #vector-th (the heading is what the pilot will actually fly — the glow makes it the first thing the eye lands on without changing its stroke); and #wca-arc (a 0.7pt arc at r=22 from 360° to 353°, with a −7° caption at (143,119) in 8pt mono) — pilots see the WCA as a geometric quantity, not just a number in the legend.
