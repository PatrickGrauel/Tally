Design notes

Layout. Origin at (110, 475) in a 720×540 viewBox. Six rays fan from the origin: three north references (True 0°, Magnetic 7°, Compass 12°) and three trajectories (Course 52°, Heading 58°, Track 64°). All angles measured clockwise from up. North rays are 420 long, trajectories 470 — so the destination labels (Course / Heading / Track) sit clear of the arc field.

Arcs. Nine arcs in three concentric tiers link each north to each trajectory. Course tier at r=370/345/320; Heading at 290/265/240; Track at 210/185/160. Stroked 1.2pt in the origin-north's color (True #5AC8FA, Magnetic #FF6B6B, Compass #3DD68C) and terminated with a small arrowhead showing CW sweep. A 2.4pt dot at the start pins the arc to its origin-line — the eye reads "this arc swings from here to the arrow".

Correction indicators. Four small arcs near the origin call out the angular gaps a pilot actually computes: VAR (r=85, 0°→7°), DEV (r=120, 7°→12°), WCA (r=85, 52°→58°), DA (r=120, 58°→64°). Stroked 0.9pt in white@70% with a tiny arrowhead; labels sit radially outside in 9.5pt SF Mono.

Variant B. Identical geometry plus two layered additions: a wind barb glyph in the upper-right negative space (cyan staff with three feathers, oriented along the line a 280°@15kt wind would cross the page) and a top-down aircraft silhouette along the Track ray at r ≈ 380, rotated to Heading (58°) — not Track. The crab angle between the aircraft's nose and its ground track is the visual definition of DA.
